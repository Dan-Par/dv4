Specific Places for Destinations

North America

LOS ANGELES: One of the most densely populated and diverse cities in the world, Los Angeles will be my first stop of my trip around the world. Not only is it close to South Pasadena, it has many landmarks that make California known for this bustling city. It has the Hollywood hills, it has the sky scraping Downtown area,and there are the Beverly Hills. Not only does it include one of the most populous areas, it includes many people who are celebrities. My specific place to visit will be here, at The Grove. This gilded open air mall is a part of my childhood memories,having spent so much time there during the weekends.

SAN FRANCISCO: A city known for the Golden Gate Bridge,is the home of many stops that are unknown to the world, but deserved to be known. The Fisherman's Wharf includes a ferry ride from Alcatraz, has a yearly display of sea lions, and one of the best clam chowder you will have ever tasted. Speaking of Alcatraz, it has been decommissioned to a museum, displaying the rows and rows of cells that held one of the Bay's most infamous criminals. Not only that, you can rent a bicycle ride to, through, and from the Golden Gate Bridge. If the weather is nice, it will be one of the most tiring but magical bicycle ride you will ever ride.

SPOKANE: Spokane is one of the few cities in the state of Washington. It was here that I was taken to the nearby state of Idaho to the Kootenai National Forest, in the Snowberry campground of Lake Pend Orielle. It was here that I caught my first fish and caught many more, sometimes in the span of 10 minutes in between each fish! We stayed there for around 3 days and had an immense amount of fun during the trip.(keep in mind that fishing here was cheating, The lake is known to have an over-populated fish problem.)

WASHINGTON D.C.: One of the most recent place I have visited, has one of the most beautiful places I have ever visited. There is the majestic Monticello, the grand Mount Vernon, and the serene Arlington Cemetery. Not only that there are many landmarks to commemorate the history of America. The is the Washington Memorial, there is the Lincoln Memorial, and there is the Jefferson Memorial. There are also museums that hold the pieces of history for all to see. There is the National Archives, where the Declaration of Independence is kept, there are the multiple Smithsonian Museums, and there is the White House, where the president lives. All in all, there will be many things you will see that you will wish you can remember forever.

Eurasia

LONDON:Home of the Queen, it has many places I have yet to see with my own eyes. I have wanted to go to the Palace of the Versailles, the Buckingham Palace, and do many other things there.

OXFORD:This will be very short due to the closeness of London to Oxford, but I do want to tour the Oxford university.

PARIS:The city of revolution,resolution,and reform, it is one of the most beautiful cities in the world. It has magnificent architecture, booming yet subtle culture, and finally, the Eiffel Tower, pinnacle of 1800's architecture.

ROME: Home of the gods, Rome is well known to be the place of the heavens, quite literally! There are any places devoted to the Greek and Roman gods, displaying an elaborate collection of types of architecture and is center of modern government.

VENICE: The center of all pasta,(Sorry Italians!) this city is well known for a magnificent view of the coast, pasta, and serene activity.

DUBAI: The place of the rich, this city is well known for its ridiculously rich and expensive people. They are famed for their streets filled with Lamborghinis and gold, and for the high rising hotels on the coast.

Asia

SEOUL: The most famous city of South Korea, it is the center of most the activity in Korea. The humungous city is home to close to the population of Los angeles, and is diverse in food as much as well.

JEJU: The popular island of South Korea, it is the center of one of the most popular place to eat seafood. During the summer,it is packed with people, umbrella bunches so large, they can be seen from space. It is very fun to go there and mind you, it takes an airplane to go there.

TOKYO: The perfect mix of culture and the future, it is the most densely city of all of Japan. It is full of restaurants, and skyscrapers, and strange traffic lights and crosswalks. It is full of electric billboards and neon signs, like all of New York and Los angeles combined.

OSAKA: This bustling city is nearby Tokyo, but it is very different from the nearby city. It is mainly know for the street food, having vendors selling Takoyaki,or octopus in batter balls, and Okonomiyaki, a mix of noodles, vegetables, and a little meat in batter. There are many other treats, like exotic versions of American foods like green tea Kit Kats.

HOKKAIDO: This is the last stop of the trip, and it will end smooth and easy. This stop will mainly be a quiet one, for it is the center of Japanese culture, away from the bustling cities, and on a quiet coast.
